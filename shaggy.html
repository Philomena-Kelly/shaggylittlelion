The root of every living thing is a spirit, not a body. The body is the vessel, but the spirit is the essence. Most creatures, however, cannot perceive spirits except for in very subtle ways. Some of the most prominent but unseen spirits are those that dwell in forests, plants, and trees.

Some spirits have humanoid appearance while others have animalistic ones, or a mix of the two. Many even have completely different and almost alien appearances, while the more powerful spirits can change their appearance altogether. Most smaller or newer spirits have no form at all and instead appear to be small amorphous blobs. These are usually very simple minded and playful, much like children.

There are in general four classes of spirit: Minor Spirits whose physical occupancy is usually something very small and seemingly insignificant such as patches of grass or moss. Lesser Spirits Which are usually smaller plants which last only a short amount of time such as dandelions or clovers. Greater Spirits are the spirits of most other plants like trees, bushes, and ferns. They are often wiser and more in touch with this realty than the lower two, especially once they are aged. Finally, Major Spirits are ancient being who instead of being attached to a single plant, are attached to several, such as a forest. They are stoic and wise creatures and are often sought out by spiritualists.

Nymphs are spirits, usually Major or Greater, chosen by the fates to watch over forests in all that the forest might need. They are required to help all those who enter their forest who are in need of assistance, which includes plant spirits which reside their. The Nymphs must settle every dispute, no matter how difficult or how small, or the balance of the forest will be thrown off.

If the balance of the Forest is altered the plants and spirits which live there become wilted, despondent, and even altogether hostile. Although a forest can survive without balance, it will not thrive nor will it be a source of much life-if any.

If a Nymph leaves their forest for any period of time longer than maybe an hour, the forest's condition begins to decrease. If the Nymph keeps leaving, the forest will often grow in a mangled mess and lose its natural appearance, instead morphing into something which appearance is so disturbing it seems that it lacks a heart.

Since the Nymph was first a plant spirit, they remain connected to their plant of origin throughout the time that they are a Nymph. Like any other spirits, if the plant is injured or dies, the spirits does so in turn. Most Nymph's plants are stronger, larger, and older things such as Trees or sturdy bushes, however that is not always the case.

When people need help, they often find their way to the forests in which Nymphs dwell, sometimes by accident and sometimes by design. They seek help for everything ranging from health problems to relationships to even money problems, and the Nymph is required to help in any way that they can. To the people that seek them out, Nymphs are like benevolent gods and are often worshiped by them, given offerings, etc.

Most Nymphs do not or cannot feel physical or romantic attraction to others, though there are a few exceptions. Deep platonic relations with mortals are discouraged especially in "newer" nymphs, because as the mortal starts aging and comes close to their death, the nymph might be tempted to prolong their lives much longer than the fates intend. Any meddling with the fates' plans are strictly forbidden and can end with the offender's execution or some other horrible punishment.


One of the most difficult duties of a Nymph is to tell a dying plant that it is dying and to ease them in their passing. This can be particularly trying for Nymphs, however it is extremely important.

tl;dr:

Every living thing is a spirit with a body, however most cannot see pure spirits. All plants have some spirit, whether it be small and unaware of its surroundings or great and wise. Nymphs are spirits chosen by the fates to look after forests and everything inside them, including the plants and spirits there as well as travelers passing through.

The Nymphs have to stay inside their forest at all times, and must also only help people to the extent which the fates allow. Any deviation from the fates' plans can result in harsh punishment.

Shaggy is an easygoing forest Nymph who has care over a large forest of enormous plants in the mystical world of Blume.

Name: Shaggy
Species: Forest Nymph/Major Spirit.
Gender: Male
Personality: Kind, carefree, and flighty. Kindness comes as a second nature to him and he only ever tries to help people. However he is easily distracted and often not very good with verbal advice, which often leads to people becoming impatient with him.
About his character:

Shaggy is a Nymph with dominion over Candlewood Forest, a unique forest composed of enormous tree-sized plants. He stays there, gladly accepting his responsibility to the forest and awaiting people to seek him out for help. Many people find their way into the forest, and in gratitude for his help, they often give him small inexpensive gifts, as he would accept nothing more.

He collects these items and puts them in the little place he calls him. It is a naturally hollowed out tree-like plant with enough space for him to live in comfortably. The plant is his plant, the one his spirit is connected to. It bears large fruits which he gives to people in need of cures or things along those lines.

Clean vs Sloppy: Living in the wild, Shaggy doesn't necessarily have the need to be clean, but he tries to make the forest as happy as possible, and a clean forest is a happy forest.

Nice vs Mean: Shaggy by nature is very kind and caring, and wouldn't hurt a fly. Even if someone is a bad egg, he'll try to treat him like he would anyone else.

Open vs Reserved: Although he helps everyone else with their problems, he doesn't often open up himself. As he has to take care of everyone in the forest, there is hardly anyone to "take care" of him. sad :c

Leader vs Follower: Shaggy is much more of a leader, as he has to be when taking care of so many creatures. however, being hard does not come easily to him and he will often let people get away with things.

Outgoing vs Shy: Shaggy has very little problems talking to people, animals, or and spirits and in consequence is easily outgoing. However he can get a little cranky after a busy day, but he tries not to take it out on people.

Creative vs Boring: In helping people he often gives them different fruit, different advice, and different metaphors to try and assist them in the way which would be right for them.

Hardworking vs Lazy: Shaggy does his job and does it well, hardly ever skimping on responsibilities. However, he does get tired and is prone to taking naps at inopportune times.

Careful vs Impulsive: Often with small tasks he goes with his gut and acts on his first instinct, however is the gravity of the situation is great he does take more time to consider other options.

Relaxed vs Tense: As Candlewood forest hardly ever has very difficult problems, and as Shaggy can usually fix any that do arrive, he seems very relaxed and carefree with very little worry.

Intelligent vs Dull: While not necessarily knowledgeable about a lot of things, Shaggy understands things quickly and is able to catch on to difficult concepts and issues with ease.

The world of Blume exists far, far away from our own in a place almost completely unknown to outsiders. It is a large and expansive place filled with forest, mountains, valleys, oceans, and floating islands. It is full of color and magic and mystery, largely untouched by modern science and society. The people their live in clusters and tribes ranging from fifty to over a thousand people each, all with their own customs, culture and beliefs.

Although the peoples are so divided, there is hardly any wars that go on between them. Each group has their own large area of land, and are slightly hostile when others go onto it, but minor scuffles are usually the extent of the violence. There is often trades and business done between groups and many trading posts throughout the world.

The people don't just differ in culture or customs, however, but also in appearance and even species. Their is much diversity between and even within groups that it would be impossible to list them all. Their a several major and interesting places around the world, such as The Planes, The Forest of The Eternal Night, The Skylands, and Candlewood Forest, just to name a few.




The Planes are far too many and too broad to all be completely covered under one section, so be aware that these are the most common things in the planes, and definitely not the rule. The planes are vast and expansive areas of land which can spread for miles across. Many tribes and peoples call these place "home", however the most common are the Kaus, a fiercely independent but un-aggressive group.

They are mostly well built and well equipped to face dangers, however they are primarily vegetarians. They are very family-centric and protective over the young and will even take in other species' abandoned children. They Live in crude huts or caves if tribes of up to thirty individuals, though it is not uncommon for their to be more.







The Skylands span across Blume in an armada floating Islands, varying in size from a few feet to a mile or more and are homes to a large number of plants, creatures, and people. Most of them however are not populated by tribes but individual small groups or families. However, there is an exception; an unusually large conglomeration of Islands which float high above most others is home to a civilization of mostly tige-rlike winged people, however there are a few purely bird like people who also call this place home.

They're position in Blume represents what they feel is their position in life-above everyone else, constantly looking down at the world below. They have high standards for beauty, strength, and intelligence and do not accept anything less. They hardly trade or make commerce with the other tribes because they feel that they are below them and do not need anything they might have to offer.

The Forest of the Eternal Night, or The Darkwood as it is referred to by its inhabitants, is a forest unlike most others. There, as the name suggests, it is constantly night. Very few people live in it, and those who do usually live there to be alone. The inhabitants range from evil wizards to cursed wanderer, travelers looking for a God and those trying to hide from One.

The forest is thick and mysterious, giving off an unwelcoming air about it. From its dense underbrush to the bioluminescent plants and creatures that call it home, it is far from familiar to most people. There are no known tribes which live within it, so there isn't any sort of definitive culture of the inhabitants. However, a tribe of doglike people live the closest to it, and occasionally make trips into the forest for various reasons.

The Nymph of the forest is an indifferent and callous spirit, wanting nothing to do with the forest in their care. They often leave it for long periods of time, causing the plant's spirits to rely on themselves for what they need. This contributes to the forest's air of unwelcomingness. The Nymph helps people with matters of a darker nature than most, such as revenge or violence.




Candlewood Forest is a mystical place full of larger-than-life plants. One of the most interesting places in Blume, it's definitely one worth a visit. With many plants and animals completely unique to the forest, it is a one of a kind place.

The Nymphs there have been constantly kind to the forest, and according to some native mythology, that's the reason it became so immense. It is believed to have started from a small and unimportant field of weeds, but the Nymph which at the time looked after it showed it so much love that with the love, the size of the plants exploded. But that is of course only rumor, right?

A long, long time ago I had managed to trade a Shadow Hissi with a nice enough name for the RW Chauffeur. Now, the thought process of young and inexperienced me was "I have a real word now, I can trade for anything!" and consequently offered on every Unconverted, Draik, and Krawk I laid my eyes on. Though my spree of offering is all a blur now, one of the pets which I had offered on stuck in the back of my mind, and that pet was Shaggylittlelion. The other was a magma draik named Firedragon1, but this is not an application for Firedragon1. If it were, I'd have gone with a completely different color scheme.

Every time I saw Shaggy Up For Trade after that, I settled back to admire from afar and silently wish that he'd end up in a splendid permanent home. Some part of me wanted to own him, to have him as my own, but the other part of me realized that my french real word was probably one of the worst offers the pet had ever gotten.

I have always admired UC Faerie Yurbles. Everything about them screamed adorable to me-the colors, the fluidity, and the pose especially. Holding their cute little foots with an adorable smile. They always stuck out to me as undoubtedly one of the best UC faeries, although I never had felt strongly about them enough to get one.

That is, until I saw Shaggy UFA. His name, the colors, the timing-everything was perfect. Everything clicked and before I knew it, I was getting excited to make the app. I was designing pages and writing stories and I was finally inspired by a pet again.


Shaggy's design, character, and the entirety of Candlewood Forest bloomed (pun completely intended) from many ideas which previously had been wandering aimlessly throughout my mind. However, once I had the opportunity to have a chance at owning Shaggy, everything came together into and orbital system revolving around the precious conglomeration of pixels.

I mainly based his design off of his neopets form, however I took additional inspiration for his physical shape from Panda Bears, Bumble Bees, and Moon Bears (AKA Asian Black Bears, but "moon bears" is such a cute name). As I love his existing color scheme, I tweaked it only slightly slightly to make it a bit more pastel as I felt it fit better with his design. I feel like although it's different, his design still retains the adorable essence found in his neopets form.

I decided to make Shaggy a Nymph instead of your average free-flying faerie because I wanted to give him a sense of purpose, a job, something to do. Although I've had my concept for Nymphs for a very long time now, I really feel that brainstorming Shaggy's character really helped me solidify and further develop the mythology.

The concept of the fictional Candlewood Forest is drawn heavily from my very real passion and love for plants. When I was in third grade, I was given an assignment in science to make a terrarium, the concept of which was at first a mystery. And enclosed biom full of a completely stable and self sufficient ecosystem-including plants and insects-all in a jar which required little to no maintenance. I was enthralled by the idea and made several of these miniworlds which sustained themselves for some time. In fact, I still own the very first one I made-in a large Utz party-mix jar, although it has gone through many overhauls and changes. Currently, my windowsills are home to countless-okay, maybe you can count them, but there are a lot-potted plants, terrariums, and even a collection of pet slugs and snails.

All this may seem unconnected from Shaggy or Candlewood, but in truth this inspired the entirety of the Forest. The wonder and beauty of these plants-and nature in general-is what inspired Candlewood, a forest full of plants that people in the real world might normally pass over as weeds blown up to of enormous, larger-than-life proportions to show just how magnificent they can be.
